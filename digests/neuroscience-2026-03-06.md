## 1. Ancient Sea Creature Had a Proto-Brain 550 Million Years Ago
3D electron microscopy of the comb jelly *Mnemiopsis leidyi* reveals its aboral organ contains 17 distinct cell types — 11 never identified before — forming synaptic contacts with its nerve net. Researchers at U. Bergen say the organ functions as a primitive brain-like center, suggesting centralized nervous systems evolved independently more than once. Published in *Science Advances*.
- https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260305223208.htm

## 2. A Hidden Physical Force Helps Wire the Developing Brain
New research (Mar 5) shows growing neurons don't rely on chemical cues alone — the brain's mechanical stiffness actively shapes how guidance signals distribute, steering axons toward their targets. The finding adds a biomechanical dimension to our understanding of neural circuit formation.
- https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/mind_brain/neuroscience/

## 3. Intelligence Emerges When the Whole Brain Works as One
A study from Notre Dame (Mar 3, *Nature Comms*) finds that rather than discrete modules for memory, attention, or language, intelligence arises from global coordination across the entire brain. The work challenges decades of modular neuroscience and supports a holistic model of cognition.
- https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/mind_brain/neuroscience/

## 4. Accelerated 5-Day TMS Matches 6-Week Protocol for Depression
UCLA researchers report that compressing 25 TMS sessions into 5 days provides comparable symptom relief to the standard 6-week schedule for treatment-resistant depression. Some non-responders showed a 36% drop in depression scores 2–4 weeks later. Published in *J. Affective Disorders*.
- https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260224023103.htm

## 5. Roche Phase 3 BTK Inhibitor Results Strengthen MS Treatment Narrative
Roche's Phase 3 trial of a BTK inhibitor in relapsing multiple sclerosis showed positive results, bolstering the case for this new drug class. Meanwhile, Novartis's REMODEL trials of remibrutinib vs. teriflunomide in ~800 MS patients are expected to read out by April 2026.
- https://www.lqventures.com/neuroscience-video-recap-march-4-2026
